Spots

Skate Parks

  1. Are you looking for a mix of street and transition obstacles like rails, ledges, bowls, and quarter pipes?
  2. Are you looking for smooth, high-grip concrete or another clean surface that makes rolling and landing consistent?
  3. Are you looking for a park with good flow and connected lines so tricks can be chained without pushing too much?
  4. Are you looking for well-spaced, skate-safe features with solid transitions and enough room to bail without collisions?

Then these are The skate parks for You!

Venice Beach Skatepark – Venice, California

  • One of the most iconic skateparks in the world
  • Birthplace of modern street and bowl skating
  • Beachfront location with bowls, snake runs, and street sections

Burnside Skatepark – Portland, Oregon

  • Legendary DIY skatepark built by skaters under a bridge
  • Known for creative, gritty transitions
  • A symbol of skate culture and independence

The Berrics – Los Angeles, California

  • Influential indoor skatepark created by Steve Berra and Eric Koston
  • Features perfect ledges, rails, and obstacles
  • Famous for high-quality skate videos and contests

FDR Skatepark – Philadelphia, Pennsylvania

  • Massive DIY concrete park built by the local skate community
  • Known for huge bowls, heavy transitions, and unique lines
  • One of the most respected skater-built parks in the country

Lincoln Park Skatepark – Los Angeles, California

  • Top-tier public street-style skatepark
  • Rails, ledges, hips, banks, and gaps
  • Frequently used by pros filming street lines

LES Coleman Skatepark – New York City, New York

  • Historic Lower East Side skate spot
  • Featured in countless skate videos
  • Diverse obstacles and energetic city atmosphere

Beaches

  1. Are you looking for consistent, rideable waves with clean lefts, rights, or A-frame peaks?
  2. Are you looking for a sandy, reef, or point-break setup that fits your skill level (beginner-friendly or high-performance)?
  3. Are you looking for minimal wind interference, ideally with offshore or glassy morning conditions?
  4. Are you looking for safe access, manageable hazards, and a crowd level you can confidently session around?

Then these are The Beaches for You!

Pipeline – Oahu, Hawaii

  • One of the most iconic and dangerous waves in the world
  • Famous for heavy, perfect barreling waves
  • A proving ground for elite surfers

Malibu Surfrider Beach – Malibu, California

  • Legendary long right-hand point break
  • Ideal for longboarding
  • Historic center of California surf culture

Lower Trestles – San Clemente, California

  • High-performance A-frame waves
  • Extremely consistent
  • Frequent host of professional surf events

Mavericks – Half Moon Bay, California

  • Home to some of the largest waves on Earth (up to 60+ ft)
  • Very dangerous big-wave surf spot
  • Surfable mainly in winter swells

Cocoa Beach – Florida

  • Main hub of East Coast surfing
  • Known for consistent beach-break waves
  • Hometown of world champion Kelly Slater

Outer Banks – North Carolina

  • Powerful, hollow beach breaks
  • Deep offshore trench creates strong swell exposure
  • Excellent conditions during hurricane season
